About Me

I am researcher and physicist (and kind of an engineer, but not quite). I have a PhD in Quantum Photonics, a field of physics concerned with studying the quantum-mechanical properties of light. I built a machine that was able to generate quantum light in the lab.

Now I'm working in industry to try to build a photonic quantum computer; a machine that uses single particles of light to do computations. It's believed that if we can build a large enough machine, where errors occur slowly enough so that we can correct them as we go, we'll be able to perform calculations that classical computers cannot.

More generally, I'm interested in the interection between computing and photonics, and whether specialised photonic hardware can be used for bespoke computing applications.
